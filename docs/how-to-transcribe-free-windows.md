# How to Transcribe a Meeting for Free on Windows

Are you looking for a way to get accurate transcripts of your Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet calls without paying for an expensive monthly subscription? In this guide, we'll show you how to use **GOATLY** to transcribe any meeting on Windows for free.

## Why Use GOATLY on Windows?

Most meeting transcription tools charge a premium monthly fee or require you to invite a "bot" to your call. GOATLY is different:
- **No Bots:** It records your system or microphone audio directly.
- **Privacy-First:** Your recordings stay on your machine.
- **Cost-Effective:** Use the managed free tier or your own API keys for unlimited free transcription.

## Step 1: Install GOATLY

1.  Head over to the [GOATLY Releases page](https://github.com/JustinGuese/flutter-simple-meeting-recorder-transcriber-summarizer/releases).
2.  Download the `goatly-windows.zip` file.
3.  Extract the folder and run `goatly.exe`.

## Step 2: Set Up Transcription

When you first launch GOATLY, you have two options:
1.  **Managed Mode (Easiest):** Sign in with your Google or Email account. GOATLY will handle the API keys for you, and you get ~500 minutes free every month.
2.  **Open Source Mode:** If you have your own [FAL.ai](https://fal.ai) and [OpenRouter](https://openrouter.ai) keys, click the **Key Icon** in the top bar and paste them in. This gives you 100% control.

## Step 3: Record Your Meeting

1.  Open your meeting software (Zoom, Teams, etc.).
2.  In GOATLY, click the **Microphone Icon**.
3.  The icon will turn red, indicating that it is recording. 
    *Note: Ensure your microphone is correctly selected in your Windows sound settings.*
4.  Conduct your meeting as usual.

## Step 4: Transcribe and Summarize

1.  When the meeting is over, click the **Stop** button in GOATLY.
2.  The app will automatically begin transcribing the audio using the **FAL Wizper** model.
3.  Once finished, click **Summarize** to generate a concise list of key points and action items.

## Step 5: Search and Export

All your meetings are saved locally. You can use the search bar at the top to find specific keywords across all your past transcripts instantly.
